Data Retention and Deletion Policy

1. Purpose

This Data Retention and Deletion Policy establishes guidelines for the storage, retention, and secure deletion of user transaction data in compliance with applicable U.S. data privacy laws. The policy ensures that data is retained only as long as necessary for business operations and regulatory compliance while safeguarding user privacy.

2. Scope

  • All transaction monitoring and budgeting notification services hosted on Google Cloud Platform (GCP).
  • All user financial transaction data collected and processed within the United States.
  • Employees, contractors, and third-party service providers with access to retained data.

3. Data Retention Guidelines

3.1 Transaction Data

  • Retention Period: Transaction records are retained for 12 months to provide budgeting insights and historical tracking.
  • Justification: Ensures users can access past financial data while limiting long-term storage.

3.2 User Profile and Account Data

  • Retention Period: Retained for the duration of an active account and deleted within 90 days after account closure.
  • Justification: Ensures continuity of service while respecting user data privacy.

3.3 Log and Audit Data

  • Retention Period: Security and system logs are retained for 24 months for compliance and forensic analysis.
  • Justification: Supports security investigations and compliance audits.

4. Data Deletion Guidelines

4.1 Automatic Deletion

  • Scheduled deletions occur per retention timelines using automated deletion processes in GCP storage and databases.
  • Personally identifiable information (PII) is permanently erased using industry-standard secure deletion techniques.

4.2 User-Initiated Deletion

  • Users can request deletion of their data via account settings or by contacting support.
  • Requested deletions are processed within 30 days, with confirmation provided to the user.

4.3 Secure Data Disposal

  • Deleted data is permanently removed from databases and backup systems following NIST 800-88 and ISO 27001 standards.
  • Data stored in Google Cloud Platform (GCP) follows GCPâ€™s data deletion policies and lifecycle management practices.

5. Compliance & Legal Considerations

  • This policy is in compliance with U.S. data privacy regulations, including GLBA (Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act) and applicable state laws.
  • User data will not be retained beyond stated periods unless legally required.

6. Policy Review & Enforcement

  • This policy is reviewed annually to ensure compliance with evolving regulations and industry best practices.
  • Non-compliance may result in disciplinary actions, including termination or legal consequences.

This policy is effective immediately and applies to all data handling and storage practices within the organization.
